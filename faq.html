Frequently Asked Questions

We've compiled answers to the most common questions about our patented framing tool based on feedback from beta testers and early users. If you don't see your question here, contact us – we're here to help you build better.

Last updated: November 4, 2025

The KiXX Square is a compact, 7-inch aluminum framing square designed for professional framers, contractors, and DIY builders. It features patented notches, guides, and scribes for quick anchor bolt marking, rafter cuts, wall layouts, slide scribing, and roof geometry – all in one tool. It's ideal for anyone tired of switching between multiple squares and measuring tapes on job sites.

Unlike basic speed squares, KiXX integrates multi-function jigs like the snap-line for straight layouts, removable threaded pins for custom pitches (22.5°/45° locks), and etched tables for rafter adjustments (±1-3/4" on 24" OC). Our diagrams show how it handles hip/valley spacing (20-1/4" down-slope) and plywood diagonals (~53.8" on 6/12 pitch) without calculators. Beta testers call it "a square on steroids" for saving up to 70% on setup time.

It's precision-machined from anodized aluminum with a reinforced 5/8-inch spine for drop and weather resistance. It's tested for wet lumber and outdoor conditions, ensuring it withstands job-site abuse. Plus, it's proudly made in the USA with recyclable materials.

KiXX is optimized for standard 2x framing (actual 1-1/2" x 3-1/2" for 2x4s, up to 5-1/2" for 2x6s). Features like the stud channels (3/2" and 5/2" paths) and slide scribe (1/4" to 5-3/4" depths) adapt seamlessly. For larger timbers, pair it with your tape measure for quick offsets.

Simply snap the square flush to the bottom edge of your wall plate (3-1/2" heel offset) over foundation embeds. Use the 3/4-inch curved notch to pencil-mark 1/2" x 3/8" holes at 6" on-center, guided by the snap-line for straight runs on uneven slabs. Our render shows the "push with pencil/jig" for clean, code-compliant drilling.
